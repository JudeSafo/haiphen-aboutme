OpSec for Mom

Open source, Apache, and cloud-native habits for everyday security.

Last week I asked my mom where she keeps her passwords. She said, "In my head. Mostly." That is exactly why OpSec for Mom exists.

Operational security does not need to be scary or technical. It is a set of calm, repeatable habits that protect your devices, accounts, and home. Think of it like locking the doors before bed.

Why open source, Apache, and cloud-native ideas matter

Open source communities and Apache projects succeed because they are transparent, tested by many people, and improved quickly. Cloud-native systems focus on resilience and recovery, not just prevention. Those same ideas work at home:

  • Transparency: Use tools that are well documented and widely reviewed.
  • Repeatability: Simple routines beat complicated one-time fixes.
  • Resilience: Assume mistakes happen and make recovery easy.

Everyday checklists

Laptop

  • Turn on automatic updates for the operating system and browser.
  • Enable full disk encryption.
  • Use a standard account for daily work, not the admin account.

Phone

  • Use a real passcode, not a simple pattern.
  • Enable "find my device" in case it is lost.
  • Only install apps from official stores.

Accounts

  • Use a password manager so every account has a unique password.
  • Turn on two-factor authentication, starting with email.
  • Store recovery codes in a safe place.

Figure 4. Layered account security: password manager, 2FA, and recovery methods.

Smart home

  • Change default passwords on devices.
  • Put smart devices on a separate guest or IoT network.
  • Unplug devices you do not use.

Figure 5. Keep smart devices separate from phones and laptops.

Backups

  • Keep one cloud backup and one local copy.
  • Test the backup once a month.
  • Backups are recovery, not just storage.

The point is calm, not fear

Security is not about paranoia. It is about freedom - the freedom to use tech without constant worry. If you help a parent, a friend, or yourself set up these basics, you are already doing good OpSec.
